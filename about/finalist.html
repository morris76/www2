CSCU Town by Town

With more than 83,000 students, CSCU has students from all across Connecticut. Overall, 96% of CSUS students are from Connecticut, and nearly nine in ten stay in the state after graduation.

As announced late last year, the Board of Regents for Higher Education (BOR), along with the CT State Colleges and Universities (CSCU), began a reorganization of the existing community college system with plans to consolidate the 12 independently administered community colleges into a singly accredited institution by 2023, starting first with a regionalization of the campuses.

The new structure will have three regions, each of which will have a Regional President.  Following a nationwide search,  finalists have been identified for each region and will be over the next three weeks will be meeting with regional leaders, community members, students, faculty, staff and members of the BOR.

This announcement today is for the finalists in Region Two, North-West, which consists of the following four community colleges:  Asnuntuck Community College, Naugatuck Valley Community College, Northwestern Community College, and Tunxis Community College.

The Region Two finalists

Ronald G. Cantor

Special Advisor to the Maine Community College System and former President of Southern Maine Community College Curriculum Vitae (pdf)

James P. Lombella

President Asnuntuck and Tunxis Community Colleges, CT Curriculum Vitae (pdf)

Thomas G. Coley

Chancellor, Ivy Tech Community College, South Bend-Elkhart, IN Curriculum Vitae (pdf)

All three candidates will be visiting within the region the week of March 18.  Dr. Cantor will be visiting on March 18; Dr. Lombella will visit on March 19; and Dr. Coley will visit on March 20.

In making this announcement, CSCU President Ojakian expressed his appreciation to the members of the Board’s Regents Search Committee and the faculty and staff members serving on the Regional Advisory Committee for their tireless commitment to this process.
