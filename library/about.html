Benefits for All

Academic Freedom

Faculty that use OER exercise their academic freedom to select course materials, and can customize these materials to fit the specific needs of their students.

Open Pedagogy

Open Pedagogy:  provide you and your students with opportunities to spend your time and effort on work that makes the world a better place instead of wasting it on disposable assignments.

Social Justice

OER is a Social Justice issue!  As educators, we want all students to have access so they can be successful.

Editing Freedom

Since OER permit adaptation, educators are free to edit, reorder, delete from, or remix OER materials.

Quick Access

All students get access to course materials on Day 1

Contextualize Learning

All students get access to course materials on Day 1

Student Success

OER use is linked to better course throughput rate - drop, withdrawal, final grade

OERs @CSCU

Why

The OpenCSCU Library Network is a collaborative service designed to support faculty overcome adoption barriers: discovery, licensing, and implementation.

How

The CSCU Council of Library Directors have committed resources voluntarily and collectively to support faculty from all 17 institutions. Librarians from across the system, experienced with OER, will provide virtual coverage Monday through Friday from 9am-5pm to address any faculty inquiries related to Open Education Resources.

What

Faculty can explore the numerous resources on the OpenCSCU website that will help guide OER discovery and creation. If assistance is needed, faculty can start a virtual chat session with a CSCU OER librarian right from the website.

Licensing Guide

The Creative Commons copyright licenses and tools forge a balance inside the traditional “all rights reserved” setting that copyright law creates. Our tools give everyone from individual creators to large companies and institutions a simple, standardized way to grant copyright permissions to their creative work. The combination of our tools and our users is a vast and growing digital commons, a pool of content that can be copied, distributed, edited, remixed, and built upon, all within the boundaries of copyright law.
