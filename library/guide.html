OPTION 3

Create Content

Creating OER content can be as simple as creating a Word document. When determining the format of your content, try to be mindful of how others may access what you share. While many have Microsoft Word, not everyone does. A RTF file format may be more accessible than a DOCX file, just as a PPT file is easier to edit than a PDF of the same presentation.Be mindful of accessibility concerns when crafting or revising your content. Many programs like Microsoft Word have built-in Accessibility Checkers that can guide you through the steps to ensure your document can be read by screen readers and other assistive technologies

Considerations

Accessibility

Just like traditional textbooks, open educational materials must be accessible to all students.  Below are resources that help with accessibility standards for OER.

  • Open Education Consortium Addressing Accessibility
  • National Center on Accessible Learning Materials
  • Merlot and California State University OER and Accessibility

Licensing

For new work, visit Creative Commons to select a license that best suits your intentions. License Creation Tool

NOTE: A Creative Commons license is perpetual and irrevocable. This ensures individuals adopting OER that they will always have access to the selected OER.
